Physically Challenged

Persons with physical disabilities face barriers not just of mobility but of access — to public spaces, education, healthcare, and employment. In India, nearly 2.68 crore people live with some form of disability (Census 2011), yet infrastructure and awareness remain deeply inadequate, especially in Tier-2 cities like Dehradun.

Key Government Schemes

  • ADIP Scheme (Assistance to Disabled Persons) — provides free assistive devices like wheelchairs, crutches, hearing aids, and prosthetics to persons with disabilities below the poverty line.
  • NHFDC (National Handicapped Finance & Development Corporation) — provides low-interest loans for self-employment and skill development.
  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 — guarantees 5% reservation in government jobs, free education up to age 18, and accessibility norms for public buildings.
  • Unique Disability ID (UDID) Card — a single card to access all disability-related government benefits. Register at swavlambancard.gov.in.
  • Disability Pension (Uttarakhand) — eligible persons may receive monthly assistance under the state social welfare department.
💡 Tip for volunteers: Help families apply for UDID cards — many eligible persons in Dehradun don't have one and miss out on all benefits.

Underprivileged Families

Underprivileged families in Dehradun often include daily wage workers, migrant labourers from rural Uttarakhand, and those displaced by natural disasters. Food insecurity, children dropping out of school, and lack of access to healthcare are the most common challenges they face. A family earning below ₹10,000/month in an urban area like Dehradun is considered Below Poverty Line (BPL).

Key Government Schemes

  • PM Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana (PMGKAY) — provides 5 kg of free food grain per person per month to families with a ration card. Ensure the family has an active ration card.
  • PM Awas Yojana (Urban) — subsidised housing for BPL families in urban areas.
  • Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) — free health insurance up to ₹5 lakh per year for eligible BPL families. Check eligibility at pmjay.gov.in.
  • Mid-Day Meal Scheme — free nutritious meals for children in government schools (Classes 1–8).
  • MGNREGS (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA) — guarantees 100 days of paid work per year per household in rural areas; some urban equivalents exist under state schemes.
  • PM Ujjwala Yojana — free LPG connection for BPL families to stop dependence on wood-fire cooking.
💡 Tip for volunteers: Helping a family apply for Ayushman Bharat or a ration card can change their lives significantly — these are high-impact, low-effort acts.
👴

Senior Citizens

India's elderly population (60+) is projected to reach 34 crore by 2050. Many senior citizens in Dehradun live alone — either because their children have migrated to metro cities or because of family breakdown. Beyond physical health needs, social isolation and loneliness are serious mental health concerns. Elder abuse (financial, emotional, physical) is also significantly underreported.

Key Government Schemes

  • Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS) — monthly pension of ₹200–₹500 for BPL persons aged 60+. Apply at the local gram panchayat or urban local body.
  • Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana — provides free assistive devices (spectacles, hearing aids, walking sticks, wheelchairs) to senior citizens below the poverty line.
  • Senior Citizens' Savings Scheme (SCSS) — 8%+ interest savings scheme exclusively for persons above 60, offered through post offices and banks.
  • Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007 — children are legally obligated to maintain parents. Senior citizens can file a complaint if neglected; Maintenance Tribunals exist for this.
  • Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY) — senior citizens above 70 are now eligible regardless of income under the 2024 expansion.
💡 Tip for volunteers: A weekly visit or phone call to an elderly person living alone can prevent depression. Companionship is as important as food or medicine.
🧒

Differently Abled Children

Children with disabilities — including autism, cerebral palsy, Down syndrome, visual or hearing impairment, and intellectual disabilities — need early intervention to reach their full potential. In India, many families in smaller cities like Dehradun lack awareness that therapy and special education services even exist, leading to delayed support that significantly impacts the child's development.

Key Government Schemes

  • Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) — provides free inclusive education, trained resource teachers, assistive devices, and transport support for children with disabilities in government schools.
  • National Trust Act, 1999 — governs welfare of persons with autism, cerebral palsy, mental retardation, and multiple disabilities. Families can apply for a Legal Guardian through the National Trust.
  • NIEPID / NIEPVD / NIMH — National institutes under the Ministry of Social Justice offer free/subsidised diagnosis, therapy, and training. NIEPVD is physically located in Dehradun (Rajpur Road).
  • Disha Early Intervention Centre (NIEPID) — free early intervention services for children under 6 with developmental delays.
  • Scholarship for Students with Disabilities (NSP) — pre-matric and post-matric scholarships available through the National Scholarship Portal at scholarships.gov.in.
💡 Tip for volunteers: Early diagnosis makes the biggest difference. If you know a family with a child showing developmental delays, encourage them to visit Latika Roy Foundation or NIEPVD for a free assessment.
📌 Sources: Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Census of India 2011, MOSPI, National Trust India, PM-JAY Official Portal, Uttarakhand State Social Welfare Department.
